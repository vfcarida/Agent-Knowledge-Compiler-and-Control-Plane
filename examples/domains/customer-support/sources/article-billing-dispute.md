# Handling Billing Disputes and Unrecognized Charges

## 1. Initial Assessment

When a customer reports an unrecognized charge or disputes a bill, the agent must first determine the source of the charge.
Common causes of unrecognized charges include:

- Auto-renewals of annual subscriptions.
- Active Add-ons (e.g., 'Analytics Add-on', 'Extra Storage') that were not cancelled when the main subscription was cancelled.
- Family members or colleagues using the same payment method.

## 2. Agent Procedure

1. Review the customer's billing history in the CRM.
2. Identify the exact product or service associated with the disputed charge.
3. Explain the charge clearly to the customer. (e.g., "This charge was for the annual renewal of your Extra Storage add-on, which remained active after you cancelled the main Premium subscription.").
4. If the customer still wishes to dispute the charge and demands a refund, assess eligibility based on the standard 30-day refund policy.

## 3. Policy on Fraud / Chargebacks

If a customer explicitly states the charge is fraudulent (stolen card) or threatens a bank chargeback:

- Immediately escalate the ticket to the Trust & Safety team (Tier 3).
- Do not process a standard refund. Trust & Safety must investigate the transaction to prevent systemic fraud.
- Inform the customer: "I take this report of fraud very seriously. I have escalated this immediately to our Trust & Safety team, who will investigate and secure your account."
